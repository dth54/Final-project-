Nutrition & Cognitive Performance

Why nutrition matters for thinking

What you eat affects how your brain gets steady energy, regulates mood, and recovers from stress or study sessions. Small, consistent choices make a big difference.

Key ideas

  • Energy & glucose: Combine whole carbs with protein to avoid crashes.
  • Protein & satiety: Protein at meals helps steady attention and reduces overeating.
  • Healthy fats: Omega-3s support brain function and may help sleep quality.
  • Hydration: Even mild dehydration reduces focus — sip water regularly.
  • Sleep & recovery: Sleep is essential for memory and mood; nutrients like magnesium and vitamin D relate to sleep quality.

Quick practical tips

  1. Start with a protein + whole grain at breakfast (e.g., yogurt + oats).
  2. Bring a water bottle and sip often during study or work.
  3. Include a serving of fatty fish, nuts, or seeds a few times per week for omega-3s.
